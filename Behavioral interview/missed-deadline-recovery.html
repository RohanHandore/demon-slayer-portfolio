Missed Deadline Recovery

Behavioral Interview Guide

Interview Question: Tell me about a time you missed a deadline. How did you handle it?

Deadlines get missed—it happens. Interviewers want to see how you communicate, take responsibility, and recover. The worst thing is a surprise miss; the best thing is proactive communication and a clear recovery plan.

What Interviewers Are Looking For

  • Early Communication: Did you flag the risk early?
  • Ownership: Did you take responsibility?
  • Recovery Plan: Did you present a path forward?
  • Learning: Did you prevent future misses?

STAR Framework

S - Situation

What was the deadline? Why was it at risk?

A - Action

  • Communicate early when you see the risk
  • Present options (scope cut, timeline extension, resource ask)
  • Own the miss—no blame, no excuses
  • Create and execute recovery plan

R - Result

Successful recovery and process improvements.

✓ Strong Answer

"I realized a week out that we'd miss our deadline due to an underestimated integration. I immediately scheduled a meeting with stakeholders, presented options (cut scope vs. delay 1 week), and recommended the delay with a detailed catch-up plan. They appreciated the early warning and chose the delay. I delivered on the new date and added buffer time estimation to my future projects."
